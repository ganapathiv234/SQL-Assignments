# SQL-Assignments
This project explores an e-learning platform database using SQL for schema design, data insertion, and analytical queries. It includes a visual dashboard summarizing key insights from learner behavior, course performance, and revenue trends.

📊 Course Pricing & Categories
•	Technology courses (Power BI, Python Fundamentals) are the most expensive, driving higher average and total prices.
•	Arts and Finance courses are priced lower, making them more accessible but contributing less to overall revenue.

👩‍🎓 Learner Purchase Behavior
•	Every learner has purchased at least one course, but spending varies widely.
•	Aaliya (India) and Chen Wei (China) stand out for purchasing multiple courses across categories, showing cross-domain interest.
•	Some learners (e.g., Goldina Thomas, Germany) bought only one course, indicating more focused preferences.

💰 Spending Insights
•	Top spenders are those who purchased higher-priced technology or finance courses in larger quantities.
•	Learners from different countries show diverse spending patterns, with India and China contributing significantly to multi-category purchases.

📈 Course Popularity
•	Python Fundamentals and Financial Accounting are among the most purchased courses, reflecting demand for both technical and finance skills.
•	Creative Writing has fewer purchases, suggesting lower demand compared to business/technology courses.

🏆 Top 3 Courses by Quantity Sold
•	Courses with higher unit prices (like Python Fundamentals) still rank high, showing strong demand despite cost.
•	Business and Finance courses also appear in the top 3, highlighting balanced interest across domains.

📂 Category-Level Revenue
•	Technology generates the highest revenue, driven by both price and demand.
•	Business and Finance categories contribute meaningfully, while Arts lags behind in revenue.
•	Unique learner counts show that multiple learners engage with each category, but Technology dominates in both reach and revenue.

🔄 Cross-Category Engagement
•	Several learners purchased from more than one category, showing diverse learning interests.
•	This indicates potential for bundled offerings (e.g., combining Technology + Business courses).

🚫 Unpurchased Courses
•	At least one course (depending on your dataset, e.g., if any course didn’t appear in purchases) remains unpurchased, highlighting areas where marketing or curriculum adjustments may be needed.

✅ Overall Takeaway:
•	Technology courses are the revenue leaders.
•	A few learners drive cross-category engagement, while others remain focused.
•	There’s untapped potential in Arts courses and unpurchased offerings, suggesting opportunities for promotions or curriculum redesign.
